PATIENT SIMULATOR — PARTICIPANT INFORMATION & CONSENT

PLEASE READ CAREFULLY BEFORE PROCEEDING

WHAT IS THIS TOOL?

This is an AI-powered Patient Simulator developed by the DClin programme at Royal Holloway, University of London. It is designed as a clinical training tool to help trainee clinical psychologists practise patient interactions in a safe, simulated environment.

PURPOSE OF USE

The simulator presents an AI-generated virtual patient. You are expected to engage with this patient as you would in a real clinical encounter, practising assessment, formulation, and therapeutic communication skills appropriate to your training level.

DATA & RECORDING

All conversations conducted within this tool are recorded and stored. Your supervisor may review session transcripts as part of your clinical training and assessment. By using this tool, you consent to your session data being accessed by your assigned supervisor and programme staff for training and evaluation purposes.

LIMITATIONS OF THE TOOL

This tool uses a large language model (AI) to generate patient responses. The simulated patient is not a real person. Responses may occasionally be inaccurate, inconsistent, or not reflect clinical reality. This tool is a supplement to — not a replacement for — supervised clinical practice, case discussions, and real patient contact.

APPROPRIATE USE

This tool is for authorised students of the DClin programme only. You must not share your login credentials with others. You must not attempt to use this tool for any purpose outside of your clinical training.

WELLBEING

If at any point you find content generated by this tool distressing, please stop the session and speak with your supervisor or personal tutor. Support is available through the programme and the University's student wellbeing services.

CONTACT

If you have any questions or concerns about this tool, please contact the programme team at psytechoperations@rhul.ac.uk.
